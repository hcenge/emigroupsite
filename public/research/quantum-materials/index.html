Quantum Materials Reveal Hidden Symmetries

Materials

Project Overview

In this line of research we probe correlated electron systems using high-resolution resonant X-ray scattering. By tuning photon energy across the absorption edge we can selectively excite orbital configurations and track the resulting emission spectrum.

Key Findings

  • Hidden rotational symmetries emerge as the material transitions from a metallic to an insulating phase.
  • A symmetry-protected gap explains the unexpected resilience of the ordered state against thermal fluctuations.
  • The experimental data matches our ab-initio simulations within 3%, validating the theoretical model.

Next Steps

We are expanding this technique to heterostructures where interfacial effects may stabilize novel quasiparticles. Collaborators interested in joint experiments should contact us.
