Neutron Imaging Captures Battery Interfaces in Motion

Energy

Using the high-flux beamline at SNS we captured 3D volumes of working solid-state batteries every 20 seconds. The dataset highlights how dendrites nucleate at voids created during cycling rather than at grain boundaries.

We correlate these observations with electrochemical impedance spectroscopy to propose a microstructural model that accurately predicts failure onset. A follow-up study will explore how interface coatings suppress the instability.
